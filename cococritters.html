CoCoCritters

For my packaging and branding class, we were tasked to create redesign a brand or create our own to make packaging for. I did the VTSU Wellness Center for my brand and logo redesign, and I didn't really know what to do for packaging for them, so I changed gears and created my own chocolate bar brand, CocoCritters. The brand's mission is based Tony's Chocolonely, which if you don't know, is big on talking about the issues in the chocolate industry, more specifically with all of the child labor going on behind the scenes, and as a result, makes sure their chocolate is 100% ethically soruced. I also wanted to have a focus on all-natural ingredients, so I knew I wanted my design to reflect that some how.

I spent a long time trying to figure out how I wanted to go about this, since I knew I wanted to bring in my simplistic style into it, but compared to a lot of other brands, there wasn't much to go off of. When looking up cute food packaging, the most common thing to show up was baby food packaging, and to my surprise, I actually got incredibly motivated from it.

The other big part of this project was rendering our product as a 3D model. Having never done this before, it was a huge learning curve. While I thought we would using something like Blender, we used Adobe Dimension instead, which isn't exactly the greatest 3D modeling software in the world, especially sine Adobe doesn't support it anymore, as they are transitioning over to Substance.
