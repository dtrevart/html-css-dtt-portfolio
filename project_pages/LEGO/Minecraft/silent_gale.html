A Silent Gale

July 2026 // LEGO

A gust confined to silent halls; A spirit caged in stone;
Forever called to battle; Yet never truly home.

This model was made for Cheesey Studio's LEGO Minecraft build competition in July 2026. The prompt was to build a place where a mob of your choosing resides, whether it be a mob house like the official sets, a biome, a realm, or anything you could imagine. My model focuses on the Breeze, and explores a question I kept coming back to while playing Minecraft: why is the Breeze underground?

The Breeze is essentially Minecraft's wind elemental, serving as a counterpart to the Blaze. While Blazes inhabit the fiery Nether, the Breeze spends its existence imprisoned within ancient Trial Chambers underground. I imagined that, despite its duty to guard these forgotten halls, it still longs for the place where wind truly belongs: the open sky. That idea became the foundation of this model.

The centerpiece is inspired by the Creator (Music Box) music disc. While the Breeze dreams of freedom above the clouds, it never forgets where it came from. The copper chest serves as both a keepsake and a music box, preserving a miniature Trial Chamber inside. Although copper chests were originally introduced alongside the Copper Golem, the heavy use of copper throughout Trial Chambers, combined with its warm, nostalgic tones, made it the perfect choice. A gear hidden on the back allows the interior scene to rotate, recreating a miniature version of the official LEGO Trial Chamber set.

To capture the feeling of flight, nearly everything surrounding the chest is built in microscale. This isn't a style I use very often, but it allowed me to create a much larger world within the contest's limited footprint. By shrinking the Overworld below and surrounding the Breezes with clouds, they appear to soar high above the landscape, finally returning to the skies they were meant to inhabit.

One feature I especially wanted to include was the ability to remove the chest from the display. Doing so naturally leaves a large opening in the center, so I designed a hidden solution rather than accepting the empty space. The landscape extends beneath the surface, adding new miniature scenes, including 2 underwater, a small cave, and another Trial Chamber, while also concealing removable terrain panels. Pulling the bars on the back releases the rear wall, revealing a hidden storage compartment where those pieces can be tucked away whenever the chest is on display.

While the music box is the heart of the model, I also wanted to capture the Breeze's signature attack. Since the larger Breezes are suspended in the sky, I built the play feature into the microscale Trial Chamber below. Pressing a hidden section of the landscape launches a white stud from a concealed shooter, recreating the Breeze firing a wind charge at Alex as she explores the chamber.

I had an incredible time designing this model and challenging myself, not only by experimenting with new building techniques, scale, and creative parts usage, but by seeing just how far I could push both the contest prompt and its constraints.
