Cuppy's Baked Goods

Cuppy's began as a joke within my family after my mom got a coffee from Holland Farms, a bakery in Utica, NY. The cup had a simple little smiling cup printed on the side, and much to my family's dismay, I immediately gave it a name: Cuppy. From there, I built an entire personality and backstory for the character, and what started as a silly in-joke slowly evolved into a full-blown brand concept.

In my final semester of college, I found myself in a class where I was able to choose a project of my own. Immediately, I knew I had to take on the idea of Cuppy's and develope it into a cozy baked goods company - one that blends charm, warmth, and whimsy with clean design. I designed a full logo and a visual identity that centered around soft colors and friendly, stylized illustrations. Everything from the typography to the characters was created to make people smile, just like the original Cuppy did.

The website was designed with a desktop-first approach, focusing on accessibility and personality. I built it using HTML, CSS, and Javascript, used for making some of the animations. A responsive hamburger menu, animated icons, and custom transitions gave the site a playful, interactive feel. The homepage includes a looping video header to set the tone, followed by product highlights, brand values, and an inviting “About” section to give users a taste of the Cuppy's world. Every part of the site was designed to reflect the heart behind the brand: sweet, silly, and full of joy.
