    • Hamlet Poster Collection

    For one of my classes, we were tasked to make a poster for our theater departments spring production, Hamlet. We went through the whole play as a class to make sure we were all up to speed, then we met with our client. There were a list of requirements, being the text that needs to be included, as well as what text needed to be where. We discussed what they would like to see on the poster, both thematically and graphically, and there was a general idea of a dark toned style, with dark academia being mentioned, but also references to the Victorian language of flowers, the inclusion of a skull, and maybe a way to tie in the costumes into the design, with Hamlet wearing black with an embrodied roses on his wrists, and Ophilia wearing white, having more pops of color compared to the rest of the cast. There were other detials, especially about what other characters would be wearing, but I focused specifically on Hamlet and Ophilia.

    While we were tasked to create one poster, the idea I had really called for 2 to work together and tandem. I wanted to completely contrasting posters, but when put together, create one whole skull. I originally started with just the darker one, but I felt like it was really basic and kind of stereotypical in terms of Hamlet posters, so I really wanted to try something different.


    When creating designs, I tend to stick to a few different methods, mainly being through building vectors in Illustrator, using stock imagery in Photoshop, or making a page layout in InDesign. While there are a couple examples of some creative work I did in Photoshop via brushes, I wouldn't really consider myself an artist in the traditional sense. I really am a graphic designer at heart, but I want to be able to use all of the tools at my desposal, so I want to get better at digital painting.
